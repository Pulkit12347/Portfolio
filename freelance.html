Freelancing in WebDev

Freelancing in web development has been a very fast-paced and rewarding journey. I worked on building static affiliate websites using HTML, CSS, JavaScript, and frameworks like Bootstrap and Tailwind CSS. These projects weren't just about writing code but about creating clean, responsive, and user-friendly websites that helped brands promote products and services more effectively.


Most of the projects I worked on were affiliate-based static websites—landing pages designed for marketing specific products through third-party affiliate programs. The challenge was in the detail: pixel-perfect design, lightweight responsiveness, fast loading times, and seamless navigation. I learned to prioritize both functionality and aesthetics in equal measure.


One of the best parts of freelancing was the collaborative aspect. I had the opportunity to work alongside other developers, designers, and project managers. We coordinated timelines, divided tasks, and regularly synced on feedback. I learned the value of delivering not just a finished product, but a well-crafted experience—one that aligned with the client’s vision and goals.


Meeting deadlines was a key part of this journey. Every project had its own timeline, and often, there were last-minute design changes or content tweaks. These moments gave me a taste of working under pressure, organize my workflow efficiently, and push through long hours when necessary. It was demanding, but also deeply fulfilling to see a live site go up and know I had built it from scratch.


Freelancing in web development was a course in adaptability, teamwork, and professional growth. I walked away with a stronger skill set, a more refined work ethic, and a clearer sense of the kind of developer I want to become. Whether it's for clients, collaborators, or users, I’ve learned that thoughtful, well-built websites have the power to leave a lasting impact and I’m excited to keep building.
